### Voice observations

1. **Outcome-led, not craft-led.** Copy leads with "clients", "results", "revenue", "leads" — not with cinematography craft language. Even the LUT product page sells the *look* ("filmic", "vibrant", "cinematic"), but the agency pages sell pipeline (leads, conversions, ROI).
2. **Second-person + we.** "We create compelling visual content that turns attention into clients" — agency assumes the work, founder-led but plural voice. No "I" first-person until you reach Dante's name in testimonials.
3. **Repeating triplet structure.** Almost every hero subhead lands on a triplet: "attract attention, build trust, and convert viewers into clients" / "educate, inspire, and influence" / "driving sales, increasing brand awareness, or building customer loyalty". Familiar agency cadence.
4. **Service-business positioning.** Testimonials emphasize "Sarasota", "homeowners", "homes", "clinic", "ads" — they're aimed at *local owners of growing service businesses* (real estate agents, painters, chiros, gyms, dentists, resorts). Not enterprise.
5. **No hype words.** No "10x", no "secrets", no "guaranteed", no exclamation marks in body copy (only on a couple CTAs). The tone is calmly confident.

## Brand voice for the new build

**Should we mirror or evolve?**

Mirror the **outcome-led, no-hype, second-person-plural** voice. It's working for the existing site (testimonials are unusually rich for a site this small). But the new build is a **vertical-specialized agency**, so we evolve in these specific ways:

1. **Replace generalist "content" with vertical-specific nouns.** "Visual stories that deliver results" → "Listing videos that close sales" (real-estate); "Brand content that owns the feed" (fashion); "Service-pro reels that book the calendar" (home-services). The structure stays — the noun gets specific.
2. **Lead with the result the buyer measures.** Real-estate: days-on-market or close rate. Fashion: engagement / collab opportunities. Home-services: booked calls / form fills. Cravecollective.co keeps it generic ("ROI") — we get specific per vertical.
3. **Keep the founder-led plural ("we"), not first-person ("I").** Even though Dante is the operator, "we" reads as a real shop, not a solo freelancer. Maintain that.
4. **Keep the calm tone — no exclamation marks in body, no emojis, no "10x" / "secret" / "guaranteed".** That's what makes cravecollective.co feel premium relative to the typical local-video-agency.
5. **Drop the placeholder leftovers.** "Make it stand out." on the home page is an unedited Squarespace block — we won't carry that pattern. Every block on the new build is intentional.

### Voice rules for new build
- Triplet cadence in subheads ("attract / build / convert")
- No first-person until testimonials
- Outcome verbs ("close", "book", "convert", "fill") over craft verbs ("film", "shoot", "edit")
- Two-sentence body blocks max
- "Book a Game Plan Call" remains the primary CTA (it's already memorable + working)
- Footer keeps the exact "© Copyright Crave Collective 2025 - All Rights Reserved" treatment — minimal, no nav cruft
